The term derived from the unrelated "French New Wave" and was originally used as a catch-all for the music that emerged after Punk Rock, including punk itself, but may be viewed retrospectively as a less challenging counterpart of Post-Punk. Although new wave shared punk's DIY-philosophy, the artists were more influenced by the lighter strains of 1960s pop while opposed to mainstream "Corporate" rock, which they considered creatively stagnant, and the generally abrasive and political bents of punk rock
