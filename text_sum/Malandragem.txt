The exponent of this lifestyle, the malandro (masculine adjective), or "bad boy" (rogue, hustler, rascal, scoundrel, gangster), has become significant to Brazilian national identity as a folk hero or, rather, an anti-hero.This lifestyle can be seen portrayed in the character "Agostinho Carrara", from the Brazilian tv show "A Grande Família". 		 	 Malandragem is a Brazilian term for a lifestyle of idleness, fast living and petty crime – traditionally celebrated in Samba lyrics, especially those of Noel Rosa and Bezerra da Silva
