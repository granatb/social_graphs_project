Sanrio's most popular icon, Hello Kitty, has become one of the world's best-selling brands. Sanriocore focuses on the more popular Sanrio characters such as:\n\n*Hello Kitty\n*Pompompurin\n*My Melody\n*Kuromi\n*Little Twin Stars\n*Cinnamoroll\n*Wish Me Mell\n*Pochacco\n*Tuxedosam\n*My Sweet Piano\n*Gudetama\n\n==Visual==\nSanriocore crosses over into multiple aesthetics such as [[Traumacore]] (the aesthetic can be used as a form of vent art in this case) but is closely related to [[Kidcore]] and [[Softie]]. It focuses on a gentle, childlike innocence and nature, accompanied by the cute Sanrio merch and characters.\n\n* Sanrio stickers\n*Pastel filters\n*Cherry blossoms\n*Sparkles\n*Rounded fonts\n*Speech bubbles\n*Pixelart\n*Desserts\n*Candy\n*Plushies\n\n==Fashion & Makeup==\nSanriocore fashion pulls some elements from [[Pastel Goth]], [[Animecore|Yanderecore]], [[Softie]], and [[Larme Kei]]. Some fashion in this aesthetic can include:\n\n*Animal ear hairbands\n*Bow clips\n*Face stickers\n*Heavy blush\n*Bandaids\n*Sanrio print tops\n*Character-themed lolita outfits\n*Pastel sailor uniforms\n*Garters\n*Lace ankle socks\n*Loose socks\n*Platform shoes (mary janes, creepers)\n*Plush slippers\n*Small backpacks\n*Character goods (plushies, badges, etc)\n\nNeutral looks with heavy blush can be seen, as well as pink, glittery eyeshadow with fake lashes. Pink lips or lip gloss with/without glitter seem popular as well. Nails tend to have heavy Sanrio deco.\n\n==Resources==\nExternal links to help get a better understanding of this
