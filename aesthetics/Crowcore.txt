This aesthetic focuses mainly on nature, environmentalism, crows and collecting (usually shiny) objects from nature and urban environments. Crowcore is generally darker than either of its initial inspirations.  \n\n\n\nCrowcore is sometimes used as an alternative to Goblincore, which has been criticized as anti-Semitic<ref>[[Goblincore#Controversy]]</ref>, but which shares the value of collecting small, lost, broken, thrown away, or conventionally trivial objects, and nature visuals.  \n\nThere is significant Tumblr cross-tagging between Crowcore, Gremlincore, Cottagecore, and various other related aesthetics.   \n== Visuals ==\nSome of these can include:\n* Old coins\n* Feathers\n* Tiny glittery pebbles/stones\n* Bones/Animal skulls\n* Rusty bottle caps\n* Tiny sculptures (usually of birds or other forest friends)\n* Vintage brooches/lockets\n* Seaglass\n* Items with crows present on them\n*Agates\n*Shiny sharp things like swords, knives, or daggers\n\n== Activities ==\nActivities include (but are not limited to):\n* Sewing\n*Dressmaking\n* Collecting\n* Wading in creeks looking for frogs or salamanders\n* Organize your collections (putting them in pretty containers)\n*Reading\n*Studying\n*Making charcoal drawings\n*Exploring\n*Spending time with friends\n*Playing D&D\n\n<br />\n\n== Books ==\n\n*''A Wrinkle in Time'' by Madeline L\u2019Engle\n*''And Then There Were None'' by Agatha Christie <br />[[File:Tumblr 56e3dd4c71b6cc0a868093c0bcfc7524 6c4f52e0 1280.jpg|thumb]]\n\n== Music ==\n\n* [https://youtube.com/playlist?list=PL9_vWyCMk5LSBWXQBF4NOVW7Si-zIgbqh Daughter]\n* [https://youtube.com/watch?v=xXGhV3ZsLms&list=PLPaztBWnatcg1vVbSwCUnLcdJi_P Lord Huron]\n\n<br />\n== Fashion ==\nCrowcore fashion is similar to [[Cottagegore]] and [[Goblincore]].
