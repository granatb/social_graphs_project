It lasted from 1910 to 1939, though it appeared to begin in the 1870s and more accurately ended in 1945, after the atomic bomb was dropped.<ref>https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SozfIGPf58o</ref> This led to [[Postmodernism]].\n\nModernism was a rejection of tradition and the Enlightenment values that had once consumed Europe. People started to feel like ideologies (religious and moral) and culture were falling apart, which went against Enlightenment ideals; Modernism embraced this fragmentation of culture. This means that Modernism can also be seen as a state of mind that continues to this day.\n\n== Art ==\nIn art, Modernism does not represent one particular style. It valued a rejection of traditional techniques. This meant a rejection of excessive ornamentation, the use of asymmetrical design, an analytical approach to the function of buildings, and innovation. The use of new materials - including reinforced concrete, curtain walls, steel frames, and ribbon windows - was also a part of Modernism. \n\nModernism in architecture includes [[De Stijl]] and Bauhaus.\n\n=== Architects ===\n\n* Marcel Breuer\n* Le Corbusier\n* Walter Grobius\n* Ludwig Mies van der Rohe\n* Kenzo Tange\n* Frank Lloyd Wright\n\n== Music ==\nModernism in music included a rejection of tonality, meter, and all else that was traditional.\n\n=== Composers ===\n\n* Bela Bartok\n* Aaron Copland\n* Claude Debussy\n* George Gershwin\n* Philip Glass\n* Percy Grainger\n* Paul Hindemith\n* Charles Ives\n* Oliver Knussen\n* Franz Liszt\n* Gustav Mahler\n* Olivier Messiaen\n* Francis Poulenc\n* Sergei Prokofiev\n* Maurice Ravel\n* Erik Satie\n* Arnold Schoenberg \n* Alexander Scriabin\n* Dmitri Shostakovich\n* Igor Stravinsky\n* Karol Szymanowski\n* Anton Webern\n\n=== Playlists ===\n\n* [https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLQxm1fp_kE924bryU8FwzX1qzYSWHU1qo Modernism]\n* [https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLC6N-PxjNhN6QT51el-5irtWloRrj80vf Modernism (music)]\n* [https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL2bSpbRqNXIhe2Ocb1ydzNNZu1xxz4mT- Modernism Songs]\n* [https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLNEyKa7f8wiGlOBJyLRaeLFeoQlV67AUO Music and Modernism]\n\n== Writing ==\nLike all aspects of Modernism, writers also focussed on a rejection of tradition.
