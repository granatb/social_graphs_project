The term was coined by an unknown Tumblr user. The aesthetic is popular with fairykin.\n\n==Visuals==\nPhotos used in this aesthetic are often edited to have soft but vibrant colours. A blur and/or sparkle effect is also very common. Small amounts of collaging may be used to paste wings, flowers, mushrooms, etc onto the photo subject.\n\n====== Motifs included in this aesthetic : ======\n\n*The forest\n*Trees\n*Fields, especially fields of flowers\n*Swamps\n*Tall, thick grass\n*Mushroom\n*Fairy rings\n*Traditionally cute or gentle forest animals\n* Rivers, streams, and creeks\n*Flowers, especially tall flowers\n*Nature paths\n*Overgrown buildings\n*Gardens\n*Cozy cabins\n*Clotheslines\n*Butterflies\n*Picnics\n**Baskets\n**Jam jars\n**Tea\n*Throw blankets\n*Gingham\n\n==Fashion==\nFairycore fashion includes:\n\n*Wire-rimmed glasses\n*Bows and ribbons\n*Lace and sheer fabrics\n*Dresses\n*Glittery, shimmery, or glowing makeup\n*Slippers, open-toe shoes, or sometimes no shoes at all\n*Hair worn down or in loose hairstyles such as messy buns and quick/messy braids\n*Pastel painted nails or no polish at all, occasionally dirt beneath nails to reference gardening\n*Jewelry like long gold necklaces and moonstone/opal rings and bracelets\n*Corsets, especially green or white (more popular with people on tiktok who consider themselves fairycore)\n\n==Hobbies==\nFairies, or people who are fairycore, often have nature-related or crafty hobbies such as:\n\n*Going for walks\n*Picking wild berries such as raspberries and blueberries\n*Pressing flowers\n*Making flower crowns\n* Playing harmless pranks on people, such as [https://www.urbandictionary.com/define.php?term=apple%20pie%20bed apple-pie beds], in the case of mischievous fairies\n*Scavenging or searching for shiny objects\n*Gardening\n*Embroidery, sewing, or knitting\n*Reading\n*Writing\n*Drawing\n\n==Similar Aesthetics==\nFairycore overlaps significantly with [[Cottagecore]] and
