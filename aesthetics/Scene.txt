Members of the original Scene subculture were nicknamed ''Scene kids'', ''Scenesters,'' or ''Trendies''. Scene is often confused with or used interchangeably with [[Emo]], a subculture that formed from the music genre \"emotive hardcore\". Music connected to Scene, includes crunkcore, deathcore, metalcore, hardcore, screamo, pop punk and post-hardcore, most notably.<br />\n\n\n\n\n\n\n== Fashion ==\nScene fashion changed through the years and will continue to depending on what's popular at the moment. In the mid-2000s scene kids would wear clothing that wasn't popular at the time, and as scene became more mainstream, the fashion became more exaggerated. Scene boys would wear clothing that was considered feminine, such as tight clothing and eyeliner, and girls would wear clothing that was not seen as popular at the time. \n\nPopular items among scene kids include:<br />\n\n* Bright, patterned clothing\n* Converse and vans shoes\n* Tight clothing\n* Hair bows\n* Flat ironed, teased or spiked hair (mostly dyed black or blonde)\n* Franchises and characters that were popular when scene was at its peak (for example, ''Gloomy Bear, Domo'' and ''Invader Zim'')\n* Raccoon-eye eyeliner\n* Striped raccoon tails\n* Plastic Pearl necklaces\n* Oversized sunglasses\n* Skinny jeans\n* Studded belts\n* Band t-shirts\n\n== Music ==\nLike the fashion, scene music changed through the years as well.
