It is about the visual; looking like a princess, but also the behavior: being poised, educated and well-mannered, but most importantly, having all the qualities to take reign.\n\n\n== Fashion ==\nAs one may expect of an aesthetic so named, Princesscore fashion takes its inspiration from real royalty, especially princesses and queens, and fictional representations. The appearance of wealth and class distinction are quite important in this style.\n\n=== Clothing ===\n\n* Originally pinned and draped pieces of fabric\n**Expensive fabrics\n*** Abaca\n*** Agave\n*** Burmese, Mulberry or other silk\n*** Cashmere\n*** Cotton\n*** Guanaco\n*** Indian wool\n*** Linen\n*** Shahtoosh\n*** Vicu\u00f1a Wool\n*Knee-length tunics\n*Ankle-length robes\n*Kerchiefs \n*Floor-length gowns\n*Light, flowing dresses\n*Shawls\n*Corsets\n* Necklines\n**High-boned collars\n**Square or round\n**V-neck \n**Sweetheart\n**Deep and narrow\n**Ruffed\n**Off the shoulder\n*With lace and/or embroidery\n*Intricate patterns, often in primary colors\n*Wedding gowns\n\n=== Accessories ===\n\n* Anklets\n*Bangles\n*Crowns and diadems\n*Earrings \n*Fans\n*Gloves\n*Necklaces\n*Parasols\n*Rings\n*Rich oils and perfumes\n*Sparkles\n\n== Visuals ==\nThe atmosphere is strongly linked to the position of a princess, because it reflects the cultural traditions and various aspects of her life. Besides, this asks or answers various questions about her status and unknown future. Unlike the [[Queencore]] aesthetic, Princesscore usually doesn't make decisions or changes to the internal politics or the economy but is learning about it so that one day they can. It is worth noting that depending on the region where the princess comes from, the set of words linked to the position can change greatly. \n\nAlthough generalized, some possible, relatable things can coffee \n\n== Activities ==\n\n* Writing letters\n* Calligraphy\n* Reading\n* Drinking tea\n* Chess\n* Daydream\n* Looking at flowers\n* Explore quaint places, such as antique shops, bakeries, and
